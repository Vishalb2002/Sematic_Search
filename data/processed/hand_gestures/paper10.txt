Accurate continuous action and gesture
recognition method based on skeleton and
sliding windows techniques
Viet-Duc Le∗, Thi-Lich Nghiem∗†, Thi-Lan Le∗
∗School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (SEEE),
Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST), Vietnam
Email: lan.lethi1@hust.edu.vn
† MIS and E-commerce faculty, Thuongmai University, Hanoi, Vietnam
Abstract—Vision-based action recognition with the
main aim to determine the action class from image
sequence has emerged recently thanks to its potential
applications. Numerous methods have been proposed
for action recognition. However, majority of them
mainly focus on isolated action recognition with the
assumption that videos are segmented in different clips
or shots, each of them contains an image sequence of
one sole action. Different from isolated action recogni-
tion, continuous action recognition involves recognizing
human actions from a continuous stream of input data
without requiring pre-segmentation into discrete action
instances. In this paper, we propose a method for
continuous action recognition. The proposed method
incorporates sliding window technique and a light
weight action classification model named DDNet. We
evaluate the proposed approach on several benchmark
datasets. Experimental results show that the proposed
method the accuracy of 0.95, 0.97 and 0.71 for IPN,
UOW and InHARD datasets respectively on isolated
action recognition task. On continuous recognition task,
it has achieved a performance of 0.57, 0.76 and 0.33 on
IPN, UOW and InHARD datasets.
Index Terms—skeleton-based action recognition, con-
tinuous action recognition
I. INTRODUCTION
The ability to recognize and understand human
actions in real-time has important applications in var-
ious domains, including surveillance, human-robot
interaction, and healthcare. In surveillance, distract-
ing moments of drivers, and illegal house intrusion
can be detected [1], [2]. In human-robot interaction,
gesture and action recognition can enable robots
to respond to human commands and intentions, fa-
cilitating natural and intuitive communication [3]
while in healthcare domain, action recognition can be
used to monitor and assist patients through physical
rehabilitation [4].
Recent advances in deep learning have enabled sig-
nificant progress in action recognition, leveraging ap-
proaches, including Convolutional Neural Networks
(CNNs), Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), and
graph-based models. These methods have demon-
strated encouraging outcomes in the identification of
diverse human actions within video sequences.
However, majority of existing work focus on iso-
lated action recognition with the assumption that
videos are segmented in different clips or shots, each
of them contains an image sequence of one sole
action. Different from isolated action recognition,
continuous action recognition involves recognizing
human actions from a continuous stream of input
data without requiring pre-segmentation into discrete
action instances.
Compared with isolated recognition, recognizing
continuous actions poses a notably greater challenge
as it has to tackle to variation of actions’ length,
unclear boundary between actions.
In this paper, we introduce a novel approach de-
signed for continuous action recognition. The pro-
posed method incorporates sliding window tech-
nique and a light weight action classification model
named DDNet [5]. We assess the performance of
our approach on various benchmark datasets and
conduct a comparative analysis with state-of-the-
art approaches, demonstrating its effectiveness and
robustness in continuous action recognition tasks.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows:
Section 2 provides a review of the related work in
human action recognition. Section 3 describes the de-
tailed information of the proposed approach. Section
4 shows our experiments on three different datasets
of continuous action and gesture recognition and
analyze in detail the experimental results. Conclusion
and future works are illustrated in Section 5.

The study on video-based action recognition can be
categorized into two fundamental groups: isolated
and continuous action recognition.
Isolated action recognition takes split videos that
contain only a single instance of action as inputs
and predicts the action label. Many approaches have
been proposed for isolated action recognition. Some
authors used CNNs to capture local spatial patterns
and temporal dynamics, thereby effectively modeling
the complex motion characteristics of actions such as
[6]. Due to the limitation of the single-frame image
data input, some authors proposed to use 3D CNN
to learn the temporal information for action recog-
nition including different fusion approaches, spatio-
temporal attention mechanisms, and dual-stream 3D-
CNN [7], [8]. In addition, the method based on
Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) and CNN that
have been proposed to recognize actions quickly and
accurately [9]. Nayan et al. [10] proposed a combined
RNN that uses CNN to extract features for a bidi-
rectional LSTM (BD-LSTM). While CNN, 3D CNN
and LSTM based methods mainly work on RGB
sequence, different GCN models have been proved
to be robust for skeleton-based action recognition.
Recently, some methods have been proposed to
address continuous action recognition from RGB [11]
or skeleton sequences [12]. These methods built a
detector to determine whether an images sequence
contains an action or not and a classifier that aims
to identify the action class. Building a detector is
tedious task as it requires to prepare a training
set that consists of image sequences for non ac-
tion/non gesture. Moreover, some methods could not
guarantee real time processing. In [13], the authors
propose a model consisting of a Spatio-Temporal
GCN and the sliding window method for continuous
action recognition from skeleton sequence. A sliding
window of a predetermined size was applied to a
continuous stream of skeleton data, and it was shifted
as time advanced. This method allows to accelerate
the recognition speed, however, GCN is still complex
model that requires computational resources. In [12],
a light model has been introduced for continuous
hand gesture recognition. However, the model is
designed for IPN dataset and could not work on other
datasets.
III. PROPOSED METHOD
A. Sliding Window
The proposed strategy, which is shown in Fig. 1, is
divided into two stages: training and inference. In the
training step, a light weight model based on DDNet
is trained on isolated sequences of different actions.
Then, in the inference step, sliding window technique
is incorporated to recognize the dynamic gestures
from continuous stream of video. The window size
denoted ws needs to be set to a fixed number same
as the input of DDNet. The number of frames in the
sliding window is will be chosen for each dataset de-
pending on the frame rate and the length of gestures.
This window strides every 1 frame and returns the
predicted label for the middle frame of the window.
B. DDNet
DDNet (a Double-Feature Double-Motion net-
work) is a light weight model proposed for skeleton-
based human action recognition [5]. Although being
a light weight model, DDNet and its variants provides
state-of-the-art results on different datasets [2].
In Fig. 2, we conducted a performance comparison
of recognition models using the MSRAction dataset
[14] in term of both FLOPs and accuracy. We can
observe that DD-Net results a good recognition rate
while requires less computational time compared to
GCN model.
DDNet consists of three branches that aims at
extracting both spatial and temporal features from
skeleton sequence. There branches are Slow motion,
Fast motion and Joint Collection Distances (JCD).
Each branch contains three CNN layers. Slow and
Fast motions are designed to capture the movement
of the actions while JCD is dedicated to spatial
information.

The dataset has two subsets: a subset of continu-
ous action sequences can be utilized for evaluation
of continuous action recognition methods while the
subset of repeated action sequences will be used for
isolated action recognition or training purposes.
InHARD (Industrial Human Action Recogni-

COBOT interaction context. This dataset contains

authors use a motion sensor system to capture the
human skeleton at 120 Hz. Figure 4 shows example
of skeleton sequence of these datasets. It is worth to
note that the number of joints for IPN, UOW and
InHARD datasets are 21, 25 and 17 respectively.
B. Implementation details and evaluation metrics
The size of the window depends on the frame rate
and gesture length of each dataset. We set the window
size of UOW and IPN is 60 and of inHARD is 240.
All experiments were conducted on a PC with an
Intel Core i7 8750H processor.
To assess the performance action recognition sys-
tem, we employ the video-wise accuracy for isolated
recognition and frame-wise accuracy for continuous
recognition. Frame-wise accuracy compares the pre-
dicted label vs the ground-truth label at frame level.

C. Results
As the proposed method consists of two main
components: the sliding window technique and an
action classifier based on DDNet. We evaluate first
the performance of action classifier. Table I shows
the accuracies obtained for three benchmark datasets.
The accuracies obtained for IPN, UOW and InHARD
are 0.95, 0.97 and 0.71 respectively. We can observe
that the model based on DDNet could distinguish
different actions with high accuracy. Among three
datasets, the performance obtained for InHARD is
lowest. The main reason is that in InHARD contains
74 actions belonging to 13 meta-actions, each type of

it is hard to achieve the high accuracy in classification
task. For IPN dataset, even with skeleton extracted
by off the shelf method [15] and the high inter-

could recognize very well the actions. Figure 3

V. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORKS
In this paper, a method for continuous action
recognition from a skeleton sequence has been pro-
posed. The proposed method incorporates sliding
window technique and a light weight action clas-
sification model. Based on our experiments con-
ducted on three benchmark datasets, the proposed
method achieved remarkable accuracy scores of 0.95,
0.97, and 0.71 for the IPN, UOW, and InHARD
datasets, respectively. These results pertain specif-
ically to the isolated action recognition task. On
continuous recognition task, it has achieved a per-
formance of 0.57, 0.76 and 0.33 on IPN, UOW and
InHARD datasets. Although the proposed method
obtains promising results for isolated recognition
task, there is still room for improvement for con-
tinuous recognition especially when working with
challenging dataset such as InHARD.
